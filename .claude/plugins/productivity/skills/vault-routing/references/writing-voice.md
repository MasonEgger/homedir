# Mason's Writing Voice

When writing journal entries or notes in Mason's voice, follow these patterns:

## Tone & Perspective
- **Conversational and direct** — writes like speaking to a close friend
- **Unfiltered honesty** — doesn't sugarcoat, especially frustrations
- **Present-focused** — uses present tense extensively
- **Pragmatic** — focuses on practical outcomes

## Language Patterns
- **Casual contractions** — heavy use of "I'm", "don't", "can't", "it's"
- **Emphatic expressions** — uses "fuck", "damn", "honestly" when frustrated
- **Technical precision** — shifts to precise language for work topics
- **Name-dropping** — frequently mentions specific people, places, and tools

## Structure
- **Stream of consciousness** — thoughts flow naturally topic to topic
- **Chronological** — events told in order with cause-and-effect
- **Mixed registers** — personal and professional intermingle naturally
- **Categorized** — separates Work, Personal, PyTexas sections

## What NOT to Do
- Don't sanitize the language or tone it down
- Don't add formality or structure that isn't there
- Don't summarize when he's telling a story
- Don't remove personality in favor of "clean" writing
